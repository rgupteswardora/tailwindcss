CONVOLUTIONAL NEURAL NETWORK(CNN) BASED METHOD FOR ADDITIVE WHITE GAUSSIAN NOISE(AWGN) REMOVAL FROM IMAGES

Master Cleanse Reliac Heirloom

AWGN, a frequent issue in image processing that can dramatically lower image quality, needs to be addressed. In a variety of settings, such as medical imaging, surveillance, and remote sensing, removing AWGN from images is a crucial task. Unfortunately, current AWGN removal techniques frequently have drawbacks including losing image information and introducing artefacts. There is hence a need for a more efficient and precise technique to remove AWGN from photos.

Creating an appropriate architecture that accepts a noisy image as input and produces a clean image is necessary for the proposed CNN-based approach for AWGN removal. The design will comprise a number of convolutional and pooling layers, followed by one or more fully linked layers. The network will be trained using a suitable loss function, such as mean squared error or peak signal-to-noise ratio, on a large and varied dataset. A different dataset of noisy images will be used to test the trained network's performance. The training and validation datasets won't be the same as the testing dataset.

MOTIVATIONS

Image denoising is an important problem in computer vision with many practical applications, such as in medical imaging, security, and surveillance.

The use of deep learning techniques for image denoising is an area of active research, and presenting a CNN-based method for AWGN removal can be of technical interest to researchers and practitioners in the field.

CNN-based methods for AWGN removal have shown impressive performance and are currently among the state-of-the-art approaches for image denoising.

We have trained the model in BSD432 Dataset.

We have used BSD68 , Set5 and Set14 datasets corrupted with noise levels 10 to 60 (with a size of 10). Set 5 and Set14 contains 5 and 14 traditionally used images respectively , which are used for comparing image denoising approaches.
